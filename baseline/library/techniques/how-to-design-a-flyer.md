# Flyer / single-screen promo — applied rules

Core idea: a flyer is read in one glance. Every decision serves a single goal + a single audience, and the layout's job is to deliver one message and one action before the reader looks away. "Less is more" is the operating constraint, not a slogan. These rules transfer directly to a hero section or a social share card.

## The build decisions
1. **Lock goal + audience before pixels.** Decide what you're promoting, who acts, and what success looks like FIRST. Let that drive font/color/tone — don't choose a vibe and back-fill the message. A networking promo and a kids' grand-opening look nothing alike on purpose.
2. **Gather the brand kit up front, not mid-build.** Pull palette, fonts, logo, icons, copy rules from the style guide before laying out. Avoid inventing tokens ad hoc — match what already exists so the unit reads as part of the brand.
3. **Ship the essential payload, nothing more.** Include exactly: clear headline, brief description, one specific CTA, contact info, and location/time if relevant. Cut anything else — extra text suppresses reading and action.
4. **Make hierarchy do the navigating — use 4 levers.** (a) *Size* — biggest = most important (headline / CTA). (b) *Placement* — put the key message where the eye lands first (upper third or center). (c) *Order* — sequence blocks by priority (e.g. event date/time before contact). (d) *Contrast* — bold weight or accent color, used sparingly, to spike one focal point. Don't fight these levers against each other.
5. **Cap fonts at one or two complementary families.** One for display/headline, optionally one for body. More than two reads as noise and costs readability.
6. **Use a limited, on-brand palette and guarantee text/background contrast.** Keep text, icons, and visuals on one harmonious palette; verify the headline survives against its background before anything else.
7. **Quality over quantity on imagery — zero images beats bad images.** A clean text-only unit beats one carrying blurry, low-res art. Never ship a stretched or pixelated asset to fill space.
8. **Refine with fresh eyes against one question: does the main message stand out at a glance?** If no, adjust size, placement, or contrast — that's the lever set. Run an actual contrast check, don't eyeball accessibility.
9. **Proof everything that can be wrong: spelling, grammar, dates, contacts, and that every link opens.** A typo or dead link kills credibility on a promo unit. Triple-check, then verify links live.
10. **Match the export format and color space to the channel.** Digital share → RGB, PNG/JPG, sized to the target platform. Print → CMYK PDF, letter (8.5 x 11 in). Wrong color space or format degrades the output quality.
11. **The one-CTA rule is absolute.** Ask the reader to do exactly one thing (attend / subscribe / buy). Competing CTAs split attention and lower action on both.
12. **Test comprehension on a real human.** Have someone glance for a second and say what it's about. If they can't, the hierarchy failed — re-present the information, don't just restyle it.

## Build checklist
- [ ] One goal + one audience named, and they drove the font/color/tone choices?
- [ ] Payload limited to headline + description + ONE CTA + contact + (time/place)?
- [ ] Hierarchy clear via size/placement/order/contrast — main message wins at a glance?
- [ ] Two fonts max, on-brand limited palette, headline contrast verified (real checker)?
- [ ] No blurry/low-res imagery — clean over cluttered?
- [ ] Proofed text + dates + contacts, and every link opens?
- [ ] Export format/color space matches channel (RGB PNG/JPG digital · CMYK PDF print)?
- [ ] A cold viewer said what it's about in one glance?
