# Serif fonts — selection and pairing decisions

Core decision: a serif's stroke contrast determines its job. **High contrast (thick verticals + hairline horizontals) = display only**; it shatters at small sizes. **Low/moderate contrast + tall x-height = safe for body.** Pick by role first, tone second — never pick a luxury hairline serif for a paragraph.

## Pick by role (named, free/Google-available picks)

### Display / headline serifs (high contrast, dramatic — DON'T set body in these)
1. **Playfair Display** — late-18th-century drama, thick/hairline contrast. Choose for fashion/editorial headings that need classical elegance. Avoid below ~24px.
2. **Bodoni Moda** / **Didot** — extreme contrast, unbracketed hairlines. Choose for luxury, high-fashion, beauty headlines. These are the "showstopper" picks; never use for running text — hairlines vanish at small sizes.
3. **Cormorant** — tall x-height, sharp, dramatic Garamond derivative. Choose for a refined modern-classic headline.
4. **Prata** — calligraphic high-contrast strokes. Choose for beauty/lifestyle branding and invitations.
5. **Chonburi** — thick rounded retro display. Choose for playful, hand-lettered, vintage logos/posters — NOT a "serious" serif.
6. **Instrument Serif** — crisp modern serif that also reads as clean UI type. Choose when you want display character but a contemporary, non-fussy tone.

### Body / long-text serifs (designed for on-screen reading)
7. **Merriweather** — condensed forms, robust serifs, generous x-height; built for screens, legible even small. Default body pick for blogs/articles.
8. **Lora** — warm calligraphic touch, comfortable in long text AND headings. Choose when one family must do both roles.
9. **Source Serif** — pairs natively with Source Sans; clean, neutral, multi-platform. Choose for app/doc body where a matching sans exists.
10. **PT Serif** / **Noto Serif** / **Tinos** — workhorse, multilingual, neutral. Choose for content-heavy or multi-language sites needing consistent legibility.
11. **Libre Baskerville** / **EB Garamond** / **Crimson Text** — classic revivals for a literary, traditional, "book" feel. Choose for editorial/literary brands wanting historical elegance with real readability.
12. **IBM Plex Serif** / **Roboto Serif** — humanist, friendly, structured; share DNA with their sans siblings. Choose for corporate/product UI body that should feel warm but clean.

### Friendly / approachable serifs (rounded terminals, lower formality)
13. **Bree Serif**, **Aleo** (slab), **Averia Serif Libre** — rounded terminals, casual warmth. Choose for approachable brand headings and short blocks; keep Averia to titles, not body.

## Build rules
1. Match contrast to size: high-contrast serif → headlines only; low/moderate contrast → body. Test the actual hairline at the actual rendered px before committing.
2. Pair by contrast-with-balance: serif headline + clean sans body (or vice versa). Don't pair two high-personality serifs.
3. Prefer a serif that ships with a sibling sans (Source, Roboto, IBM Plex) when you need a guaranteed-harmonious pairing fast.
4. Give serifs air — generous white space and correct kerning; tight letter-spacing makes even elegant serifs feel cramped and kills the detail you chose them for.
5. Use weight + italic of ONE family to build hierarchy before reaching for a second family.
6. Verify per medium/device: a serif that's gorgeous in a hero can be unreadable as 14px body on a low-DPR screen — check both, don't trust the headline render.

## Build checklist
- [ ] Serif chosen by ROLE (display vs body), not just by looks?
- [ ] High-contrast/hairline serif confined to large sizes only?
- [ ] Body serif has tall x-height + low/moderate contrast and was read-tested at real body px?
- [ ] Headline/body pairing has contrast but balance (not two loud serifs)?
- [ ] Kerning/letter-spacing and surrounding white space checked — type not cramped?
- [ ] Rendering verified at the smallest real size and on a non-retina device?
